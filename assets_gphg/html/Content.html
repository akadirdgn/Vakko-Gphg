A distinctive platform and a prestigious yearly event honouring horological innovation, design and craftsmanship, the Grand Prix d’Horlogerie de Genève (GPHG) is making a stopover in Istanbul for the first time.

In the run-up to the annual awards ceremony to be held in Geneva on November 13rd, the nominated timepieces for the 25th edition of the GPHG will be exhibited at Vakko Hotel Sumahan Bosphorus from October 15th to 20th, in partnership with Vakko.

The GPHG’s annual travelling exhibitions provide a rich tapestry of contemporary watchmaking, showcasing creativity, technical mastery, and artistic diversity and offer a unique opportunity to discover first-hand some of the most extraordinary watches and the fascinating stories that make each piece a work of art in its own right.

ABOUT GPHG

Established in 2001, the Foundation of the Grand Prix d'Horlogerie de Genève (GPHG) is a non-profit organization with a commitment to celebrating and promoting the watchmaking art.

The GPHG notably plays this unifying promotional role by staging an annual competition involving several hundreds of watches marketed during the year and attributing the highest awards in this domain. In doing so, it contributes to enhancing the traditions, skills and values of Swiss - and indeed universal - watchmaking culture.

The GPHG awards ceremony held in November of each year draws elite representatives of the international watchmaking profession, united in

honouring the finest creations and stakeholders who daily breathe vitality and creativity into the watchmaking world. Twenty or so prizes, including the "Aiguille d'Or" Grand Prix (Best in Show) distinction are awarded on this occasion.

Each autumn, in the run-up to the annual awards ceremony, the GPHG organises a travelling exhibition stopping off in the world's major cities presenting the nominated watches. Each of these exhibitions enables a cosmopolitan public to discover and admire the most beautiful timepieces of the year, exceptionally brought together in various key locations. The winning watches are also exhibited after the awards ceremony.
